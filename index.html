Alan Mautner

About

Alan Mautner is a conductor, cellist, and educator based in Los Angeles whose career reflects over three decades of musical leadership, artistic versatility, and deep commitment to student growth. He currently serves as Music Director and Conductor of the La Mirada Symphony, Orchestra Director at the Los Angeles County High School for the Arts (LACHSA), and Orchestra Director for the Youth Orchestra of Los Angeles (YOLA).

A Los Angeles native raised in Downey, Mr. Mautner is not only a dedicated educator, but also an accomplished performer and conductor. As a cellist, he has performed with leading regional ensembles including the Pacific Symphony, Riverside Philharmonic, Redlands Symphony, Desert Symphony, and Mozart Classical Orchestra. He has shared the stage with artists ranging from Andrea Bocelli, Kristen Chenoweth, and James Taylor to Boz Scaggs, Glen Campbell, and Melissa Manchester. His recording credits span an equally broad spectrum, including sessions with Barry White, Phil Collins, Christina Aguilera, Snoop Dogg, and the Goo Goo Dolls. He has toured internationally with his piano trio Trio de Bois (including performances in Egypt and Paris), and performs regularly with his contemporary string trio, Vio-fonik.

Mr. Mautner joined the faculty at Los Angeles County High School for the Arts (LACHSA) in 1990 and is now entering his 35th year. He succeeded his father, Arthur Mautner, as director of the String Ensemble and String Chamber Music Program, and was appointed conductor of the LACHSA Symphony Orchestra in 1994. Under his direction, the orchestra has performed at Walt Disney Concert Hall, the Segerstrom Center for the Arts, and in side-by-side concerts with the LA Philharmonic. Many of his students have gone on to study at Juilliard, Eastman, Oberlin, USC, and other top conservatories. He has also conducted full operas (Carmen, The Magic Flute, The Tales of Hoffmann) and music-directed numerous stage productions (West Side Story, Into the Woods, Evita, and more). In 2013, he collaborated with LA Opera conductor James Conlon on a production of Britten’s The Little Sweep at LACHSA.

In 2014, Mr. Mautner helped establish YOLA at LACHSA, a partnership with the LA Philharmonic that brought free string instruction to fourth- and fifth-grade students in the Cal State LA area. The program has since grown to over 150 students and recently expanded to Torres High School in East LA. He currently conducts both the YOLA Torres Youth Orchestra and the YOLA Chamber Ensemble, while mentoring teachers and guest conducting at other YOLA sites including the Expo Center and Heart of LA (HOLA). His YOLA leadership was recently featured in the LA Phil’s Mahlerthon festival at Walt Disney Concert Hall, where he conducted YOLA through the fourth movement of Mahler’s “Titan” Symphony.

Mr. Mautner was appointed Music Director of the La Mirada Symphony in 2014. Under his baton, the orchestra presents four free concerts annually, as well as outreach programs for local students. He has expanded the ensemble’s reach through collaborations with artists from Animaniacs in Concert and Classic Albums Live, and continues to foster access to classical music across the Norwalk/La Mirada communities.

In addition to his primary appointments, Mr. Mautner conducts the Wildwood Music Camp Senior High Festival Orchestra each summer—a role passed down from his father—and has taught at the Orange County School for the Arts, Concordia University, Cal Poly Pomona, and Cal State Los Angeles, where he currently teaches string methods.

Mr. Mautner holds a Bachelor of Music degree in Cello Performance from California State University, Fullerton, and resides with his wife Pearly and their two turtles, Stephanie and Stewie, in Buena Park, California.
